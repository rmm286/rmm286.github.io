Masters Work — UC Davis

During my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at UC Davis I worked as a researcher on motion planning and control for non-holonomic mobile robots. I implemented and validated a Model Predictive Controller (MPC) for constraint-aware trajectory tracking, designed path planning algorithms in C++, Python, and OMPL for collision-free navigation in cluttered environments, and developed full-stack mobile robot simulation in Gazebo and ROS. The projects below are the smooth curvature planner and the dynamics model and simulation used for that work.

04

Smooth Curvature Planner

Developed planning algorithms for non-holonomic robots that respect acceleration and jerk constraints.

Implemented multiple approaches:

  • RRT*, an online NLP optimizer, and a heuristic approach based on scientific literature.
  • Backman Approach: Constructs paths from elementary segments while adhering to constraints.
  • OMPL Approach: Uses an off-the-shelf solver that incorporates state equations and constraints to generate planning outputs.

Tested the efficacy of these algorithms both in simulation and on hardware.
