Professional Experience — Osaro

As Sr. Software Manager at Osaro, I architected a Vision-Language-Action (VLA) platform for industrial robotics. I grew the engineering org from 5 to 12 and led multi-million dollar enterprise deployments (e.g. Home Depot, DHL)—owning on-site integration, hardware debugging, and architectural decisions.

On the systems side, I built real-time inter-service communication with gRPC and Protobuf for low-latency streaming between distributed planning services and edge-deployed robot controllers. I also validated motion plans using the Bevy simulation framework for industrial 6-DOF arms, troubleshooting inverse-kinematics and motion-planning failures in live customer environments.

Masters Work — UC Davis

During my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at UC Davis, I researched motion planning and control for non-holonomic mobile robots. I implemented and validated a Model Predictive Controller (MPC) for constraint-aware trajectory tracking, designed path-planning algorithms in C++, Python, and OMPL for collision-free navigation in cluttered environments, and built full-stack mobile robot simulations in Gazebo and ROS.

04

Smooth Curvature Planner

Developed planning algorithms for non-holonomic robots that respect acceleration and jerk constraints, and validated each approach in simulation and on hardware.

  • RRT*: Sampling-based planner paired with an online NLP optimizer and a heuristic method drawn from the literature.
  • Backman Approach: Constructs paths from elementary curve segments while enforcing curvature and velocity constraints.
  • OMPL Approach: Leverages an off-the-shelf solver with embedded state equations and constraints for rapid plan generation.
